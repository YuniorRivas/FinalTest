Know Your Rights: Protests and the NYPD

Understanding Your Rights During Protests

In recent weeks, college campuses across New York City, including the City College of New York (CUNY), have seen significant student protests primarily focused on pro-Palestinian causes. The protests, which began in mid-April 2024, have involved encampments and marches, leading to confrontations with the police and numerous arrests. The altercations in many of these protests highlight the importance of understanding your rights and how to interact with law enforcement.

Know Your Rights

  • Right to Peaceful Assembly: You have the right to peacefully assemble and protest. However, you must comply with lawful orders from police officers, such as dispersal commands, to avoid charges like disorderly conduct.
  • Identification: You are not required to show ID unless you are being lawfully detained or arrested. If asked for ID, you can inquire if you are being detained.
  • Recording the Police: You have the right to record police activities in public spaces, as long as you do not interfere with their operations.
  • During an Arrest: If arrested, remain silent and ask for a lawyer. Do not resist arrest, as this can lead to additional charges.
